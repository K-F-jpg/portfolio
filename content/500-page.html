"Beauty: In the Eye of the Beholder?"
Core II // 500-page book
1 500-page book
This book was created in the last month of my Sophomore year, in Core Studio 2. This was an assignment to create a 500-page book on the topic of "speculative worlds," looking into the past, present, and future of our topic. I chose to research "Beauty," exploring concepts of beauty standards between women and men throughout history. I also incorporated a bit of a fashion perspective, but my focus was primarily on beauty and its evolution to more synthetic appearances in the present day. I started from the beginning of time because I wanted to delve into attraction and appearances at a fundamental level, then worked through the most pertinent centuries and eras in beauty (Greece, Rome, 14th, 15th, 16th centuries), and finally from the late 1800s- present day by the decade. For the speculative portion, I explored AI in beauty as it's being used today, and how its role can also evolve in the future.

I used mirror paper for the cover, hand-lettering the title in lipstick; this decision was inspired by bathroom medicine cabinets, which are typically used to store a myriad of beautification products.
