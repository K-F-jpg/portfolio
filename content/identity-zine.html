Identity Zine
Multi Studio I // Leave-Behind
printer paper (20lb)
10 zines
1)
We were required to create a zine, or some kind of publication, that talked about our identities to act as a "leave-behind" for people who wanted to get to know us better; essentially a business card with more artistic intent. I took a few weeks to make a zine that reflected my digital identity as a graphic designer, programmer, and internet afficionado. On the back, I added a magenta background upon which I collaged text to form the code to access my (old) portfolio's landing page. A few features of note include:
  • The text in this zine was assembled from screenshots of memes.
  • The original project had sticky notes featuring pasted QR codes that lead to a TECHNOCORE music playlist on YouTube. This playlist is representative of a "digital landscape" and incldes sounds reminiscient of technology and digital life.
  • I used the color magenta as a reference to my prior GDES cohort name and favorite color.
On the page featuring the collaged text, I also include a link to a google doc that walks the viewer through setting up an IDE and typing in the code to achieve a functioning website. You can find this document here.
