Identity Zine
Multi Studio // Leave-Behind
printer paper (20lb)
10 zines
We were required to create a zine, or some kind of publication, that talked about our identities in some way to act as a "leave-behind" for people who wanted to get to know us better; basically a business card with more artistic merit. I took a few weeks to make a zine that reflected my personality and identity, making it resemble a computer screen/ laptop to capture my digital identity. On the back, I added a magenta background and in front of that, collaged text to form the code to create the landing page to my website.
The "front" (or main contents) of the zine consists of me spelling out who I am as a designer. The back (viewed when unfolded) displays the code that creates the landing page to my (old) portfolio site. I repurposed the text from memes...

EASTER EGGS:
  • text created from screenshots of meme text and assembled/ collaged
  • the original project had sticky notes with qr codes pasted on them that lead to a TECHNOCORE music playlist, which you can listen to using this link or on this page.
  • the text on the back uses the same method as the front text to assemble the landing page to my portfolio, that you could plug into VScode or another text editor to get my landing page. It was completed with a google doc on instructions to use text editors to code the page (which you can actually click from here!).
  • magenta background to speak to my prior GDES cohort and favorite color.
On the page featuring the collaged text, I also include a link to a google doc that walks the viewer through setting up an IDE and typing in the code to get a functioning website. You can find this document here.
